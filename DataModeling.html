Introduction

Project where loan data is used to build a model in order to predict likelihood of future user defaults.

Analysis

There are many factors that go into whether or not an individual will default on their loan. Using this model, individual predictions can be given, and with the utilization of the data, an analyst can make an informed assumption on the profitability of loans.

Figure 1 - Prediction Explanations for the light gradient boosted trees classifier with early stopping model, showcasing the 4 features with the highest impact on default rate.

By using the model with the highest sample size and evaluating the prediction explanations in figure 1, grade is predicted to have the impact on default rate. As can be seen in figure 2, based on this data, 47.69% of users with an E grade default on their loans. Comparatively, as seen in figure 3, users with an A grade only have a 3.42% default rate. While A grades will be given lower interest rates, it is a much better investment than having a nearly 50% chance of losing your entire investment with the E grade loans. Using this information, a user would be much less likely to give out a loan to those with lower grades as they have a high likelihood of defaulting.

Figure 2 - DataRobot data showcasing that 47.69% of E grade users default on their loans.

With the use of DataRobot we can see that interest rates have the second highest impact on defaulting. Looking at the data showcased in figure 4, the likelihood of default increases steadily until you get into the 18/19% category, where it becomes exponentially more likely. Using this information, any loan that requires an interest rate of around 19% needs to be more carefully reviewed as it is more risky for the company.

Figure 3 - DataRobot data showcasing the correlation between higher interest rates and higher user defaults.

Conclusion

Using the model created by DataRobot, a prediction can be made for potential applicants, figure 5. For this case, as shown in figure 6, after calculating the decision we can see that two people received over a 90% chance of defaulting which is indicated by the prediction label. However we can also see that there are two people above a 25% chance of defaulting. With these predictions, an informed decision can be hand crafted based on personal risk tolerance, giving the user the highest likelihood of getting a return of their investment.
