Introduction

Using data to understand customer insights and make changes based on those insights is an extremely valuable skill to have. With the use of RapidMiner, we can use Natural Language Processing (NLP) to get insights into online reviews and adapt the business to better meet customer needs and expectations. For this case study, a company can visualize the average score values of all the different products being sold.

Analysis

And as can be seen in figure 1, by setting a maximum rating value of 0, a company can view all of the products that have a negative average score. Using this information, a decision can be made on whether the product is worth revising in the hopes of receiving better reception, or scrapping the product entirely. Both of which give the company the opportunity to raise the overall product ratings and develop better customer relationships.

Similarly, using the Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) model, a company could see the most popular words used in reviews, this could better inform the company of customer priorities. For instance, as can be seen in figure 2, the top two words are “coffee” and “chips”. Based on this information, there is a lot of discussion about the chips and coffee that the company offers, this could indicate that there is a lot of demand for these products and more focus could go into these areas and more product lines could be introduced/developed. However this list can also help indicate improvements that could be made, at the bottom of the list.For example, some of the most popular words in this case study are: “plastic”, “time”, “easy”, and “bags”. Given that the company is focused on healthy food, the clientele are more likely to be eco friendly as well. This along with the words “plastic” and “bags” being discussed could be an indicator that there is desire for a more eco-friendly option to take their groceries home with.

Figure 2 - DataRobot data showcasing that 47.69% of E grade users default on their loans.

Additionally, the word “easy” being in the lower end of the most frequently used words could also be troubling. Using this analysis, the company could try to find ways to make the shopping experience more streamlined to better customer relations. Although it is not a guaranteed indicator that there is need for change, it would be advisable for the company to keep this in mind when it comes to future improvements.
